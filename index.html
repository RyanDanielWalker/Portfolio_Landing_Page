About me

Hello!

My name is Ryan Walker and I am currently a full-time student at Epicodus. From 2014 until 2020, I was the General Manager and minority owner of Atlas Pizza in beautiful Portland, OR. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit and it appeared that the business might not survive, I made the decision to be laid off. Initially, I was terrified, but over time I began to realize that I was not allowing myself to grow as an individual while investing, almost literally, all of my life in to work. I started attending Portland Community College shortly after to pursue my passion for art, specifically design. A few of my friends started to tell me about a computer programming course they had taken that they thought would be perfect for me. With the hopes of being able to further develop my design skills, and begin a career in which I can constantly learn and grow, I took the plunge and signed up!

Design Work

Passion Projects

I have been collecting vinyl records for more than half of my life. With all of the extra free-time I had available during the beginning of the pandemic, I decided to document and share some of my favorite tracks from my collection in the form of cassette tapes. Here is a little information about my process:

  1. My initial goal was to record at least one song from every album that I have. It immediately became clear that it was going to be a challenge to spread out my absolute favorites. Although I have, what some might consider, a lot of records; I must admit that I do own quite a few that I only have because of one or two songs.
  2. In an effort to ensure that no two tapes featured too much of any one sound, I decided to try to follow a similar pattern for each tape:
    • Start out with a banger. Usually a funk or disco track. Something high energy to let them know I mean business.
    • Next, hit 'em with another one. Often this meant another funk or disco track but sometimes a hard rock song would sneak its way in there.
    • Songs three through six generally alternated between folk, blues, country western, soul, and instrumental rock. Songs to mellow the listener out a little while still bringing the heat.
    • For songs seven and eight, I ramped up the energy just enough to get them excited for side B.
    • On the B side, I essentially repeated the same pattern as side A while saving the absolute heaviest of hitters for the final track
  3. After all of the tapes were recorded, I made custom inserts that included handwritten tracklists and a unique painting for each one.
